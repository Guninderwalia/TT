1. What "Lead" means inside TaskTango

You're a Department Lead if your admin flagged you as is_department_lead = 1 and your department has you set as its lead. In TaskTango terms that gives you four extra powers your team members don't have:

  1. See your whole team's live status — who's signed in, on break, late, absent, on leave.
  2. Approve or reject leave requests at the first stage (admin still gives final sign-off).
  3. Write performance reviews for the people in your department.
  4. View your team's time logs end-to-end.

Crucially you are also still an employee: you sign in, you log breaks, you request your own leave. Sections 1–9 of the Employee Training Guide still apply to you for those parts.

You can't approve your own leave request — that's deliberate. The system automatically routes your leave to the admin, skipping the lead step. So request leave like any other employee; admin handles it.

2. Your lead dashboard explained

The Lead Dashboard looks similar to the employee one but adds three lead-only widgets at the top:

📊 Today's Team Status (donut chart)

A live donut showing how many of your team are currently Working, On Break, Signed Off, Absent, On Leave. Refreshes every 30 seconds. Hover any slice to see the count.

👥 Team Member List with presence dots

Below the chart, each team member appears as a row with their photo, name, status pill and the time they signed in. The list is sorted by status — Working at the top, Offline at the bottom — so you can see at a glance who's not at their desk.

📈 Team Attendance This Week (bar chart)

One bar per weekday, stacked by status. Lets you spot patterns — e.g. "Wednesdays we always have 3 people late". Click any bar to drill in.

📅 Upcoming Leaves

Lists every approved leave for your team in the next 30 days. The 📅 Export to Calendar button gives you an .ics you can drop into Outlook/Google so you always know who's out next month.

⏱ My Hours Logged (last 12 weeks)

A personal chart of your own hours — leads need to track their own productivity too.

All these charts use real data from the same DB the admin sees. There is no separate "lead view" of the data — you see the same numbers, just filtered to your team. If the chart looks wrong, the underlying records are wrong; talk to your admin.

4. Team time logs — viewing & sanity-checking

Click Time Logging in the sidebar. As a lead you see two tabs:

  • My Time — your own sign-in/break/sign-out controls (same as any employee)
  • Team Logs — a table of every team member's daily totals

What the Team Logs table shows

Sign OutLatest sign-out, or blank if still in

Filtering & date range

Use the filter row at the top: pick an employee (or "All"), a date range, and press Apply. Use 📄 Export to PDF to grab a printable timesheet — handy at month-end.

You can view team time-logs but you cannot edit them. Only admins can correct mistakes. If a team member tells you they forgot to sign out, message your admin with the date and the correct time.

5. Approving leave (single & bulk)

This is where most of your day-to-day lead work happens. Open Leave Approval Hub from the sidebar.

Understanding the queue

Every row is a pending request from someone in your department. Columns: Employee · Leave Type · From · To · Days · Reason · Status · Actions. Read the reason carefully — that's your context.

Single approve / reject

  1. Find the row you want to action.
  2. Click Approve — request is sent up the chain to admin for final approval.
  3. Or click Reject. A modal asks for a reason — be polite but specific ("Two others already off that week, please pick different dates").
  4. The employee gets a 🔔 notification immediately.

Bulk approve / reject (new in v4.5)

  1. Tick the checkbox at the left of each request you want to action together.
  2. Or tick the master checkbox in the header to select all.
  3. A green action bar appears at the top: ✓ Approve Selected · ✗ Reject Selected · Clear.
  4. Click Approve Selected to approve them all in one go.
  5. Or Reject Selected — one reason is sent to every selected employee, so phrase it carefully.
Diwali week. Six people from the team have all asked for the same Thursday and Friday off. Mira opens the Hub, ticks the four she can spare, hits Approve Selected — done in 5 seconds. For the remaining two she opens each individually and writes a custom rejection: "Sorry Anuj, two leads already approved — can you shift to next Monday?"

What if you're on leave yourself?

The system shows you all pending requests except your own. If a critical request comes in while you're away, your admin can step in and approve directly.

6. Writing performance reviews

Open Performance Reviews from the sidebar. You'll see a list of your team members with their last review date.

Creating a review

  1. Click + New Review next to the employee.
  2. Pick the review period (e.g. Jan–Jun 2026).
  3. For each scoring category, pick a number from 1 to 5. See the separate Performance Review Scoring Guide for what each score means.
  4. Write Strengths — be specific. "Delivered the Razorpay migration two weeks early" beats "good worker".
  5. Write Areas to improve — frame as growth, not blame.
  6. Write Overall comments — the summary the employee will remember.
  7. Click Save Review.

What the employee sees

Their My Performance page now lists this review. They can read every category, see their overall score (e.g. "4.2 / 5") and download a PDF that includes:

  • Their name and the review period
  • Your name as their Team Lead (both in the row and as the signature line — fixed in v4.5)
  • Every category score and your comments
  • A clean signature block for both of you to sign if needed for HR records
Do reviews face-to-face. Use the chat call (audio or video) to talk through scores before they go on the record. People accept feedback far better in conversation than as a sudden PDF.
Don't score everyone 5/5 to keep the peace. It dilutes the system for the team members who genuinely deserve it. A spread of 3–5 across a team is healthy.

7. Chat & calls with your team

Same chat module as employees (see the Employee Guide for the basics), but as a lead you'll lean on it more:

Practical lead chat patterns

  • Morning check-in — quick "good morning" in each thread; presence dot tells you who's at the desk before you message.
  • Sprint stand-ups — start a video call from the chat thread; minimize the call so you can still see your charts.
  • 1:1s — schedule weekly; use video calls.
  • File hand-off — drag a PDF into the chat attachment button.

If someone's status is wrong

Presence is computed from real DB events (sign-in, break, sign-out). If someone shows Working but they've actually left the office without signing out, the auto-sign-out cron at 23:00 will correct it. For the dot to update during the day, they need to sign out themselves.

8. Org Chart for headcount conversations

Open Org Chart from the sidebar. You'll see the full company as a tree, with photos for those who've uploaded one.

  • Zoom + / − to fit on screen.
  • Drag the empty space to pan.
  • Export to PDF for the office wall or board pack.
When negotiating with admin for an extra headcount in your team, export the Org Chart PDF and bring it to the meeting. Visual headcount conversations are far more persuasive than spreadsheets.

9. Your own employee tasks

You are also an employee. Every section in the Employee Training Guide applies to you for your own activity:

  • Sign in / sign out (Quick chip in header)
  • Log breaks on the Time Logging page
  • Submit your own leave (routes straight to admin, skipping the lead step)
  • Read your own performance reviews (your admin writes yours)
  • Download your own HR documents
  • Keep your own profile completeness ring green

10. Leadership etiquette & common pitfalls

✓ Do

  • Reply to leave requests within 24 hours — bottlenecks demoralise the team.
  • Use the bulk approve only for clear-cut cases. If a leave overlaps with a sprint deadline, decide individually.
  • Document tough conversations in the chat — the timestamp protects everyone.
  • Run a quick "team status" check every morning at 09:30 to spot anyone red.

✗ Don't

  • Don't approve everyone's leave for the same week without checking — you'll end up with skeleton cover.
  • Don't write performance reviews in haste 5 minutes before the deadline — the employee will remember every word.
  • Don't share screenshots of presence dots outside the team — that's effectively private HR data.
  • Don't try to edit team time logs by going through Settings — that's an admin path; ask them.

12. FAQ & troubleshooting

Q: A team member says their leave balance is wrong

Ask them to open Leave Requests once — that re-seeds the balance row. If still off, escalate to admin (they can edit balances in the Employee Manager).

Q: My Approval Hub is empty but I know there are pending requests

You only see requests for your department. Requests from other teams are invisible to you. Also: your own requests don't appear (admin handles those).

Q: I approved a leave but admin hasn't actioned it

Approved-by-lead requests sit in the admin's Final queue. Send the admin a chat nudge — if they've forgotten, one click clears it.

Q: The team status chart says someone is Working but they're not in the office

They signed in remotely. TaskTango doesn't enforce a location check. If your company is strict about on-site presence, raise that with admin — they can enable IP whitelisting in Settings.

Q: Can I assign someone else to write a review on my behalf?

Not in v4.5. Admins can write reviews for any team. If you're going on a long leave, ask admin to cover.

Q: Can I see the org chart for a different department?

Yes — the chart shows the whole company. You can zoom into any branch.

Q: How do I know which version I'm on?
