Team Analytics on Your Dashboard

The 💬 chat icon moved to the dashboard header next to the 🔔 bell. New features:

  • Desktop notifications when someone messages you and the chat panel isn't open.
  • Auto-jump to the most recent unread thread the moment you open chat.
  • Pulsing red badge over the icon when you have unread messages.
  • Emoji picker (😊) with the most-used emojis.
  • File attachments (📎) up to 10 MB — useful for sharing screenshots, shift schedules, training PDFs, etc.

Past-Employee Awareness

When a team member is offboarded by Admin, they immediately drop off your Team Members, Team Attendance, and approval lists. Any pending leave requests they had for dates after their last working day are auto-cancelled, so you won't see them in your inbox.

Cleaner Performance Review

If you have access to Performance Review, the table now has a new Employee filter after Department so you can drill into one person at a time. The Last Increment column is gone — that data is on their employee record.

Mobile-Friendly Approvals

Need to approve a leave from your phone? The whole app is now responsive: at tablet width (≤900 px) the sidebar collapses to a top bar, and at phone width (≤540 px) the nav becomes icon-only and tables scroll horizontally so columns don't overlap.

Getting Started

  1. Open TaskTango and log in with your username and password.
  2. You'll land on the Department Lead Dashboard. The menu on the left lists everything you can do.
  3. The top of the menu is for your team. The bottom (anything starting with "My") is for your own employee actions.
Part 1 — Managing Your Team

🏠Dashboard

A quick summary of your team — who's signed in today, any pending approvals, and team performance at a glance.

What to do here: Use it as a daily check-in. If you see "1 pending approval" jump to Leave Approvals to deal with it.

👥Team Members

A list of everyone in your department.

What to do here: Check who's on your team, contact details, role, and joining date. You can't edit employee data here — that's an admin task. Use this to look people up.

📅Team Attendance

A monthly grid showing each team member's attendance, day by day.

How to read it:

To change someone's status: Click a cell, choose the correct status, and save. You can also bulk-mark several days at once.

Tip: Use this view to spot patterns — frequent absences, late arrivals, etc. — before they become performance issues.

✅Leave Approvals

This is where your team's leave requests come in for you to review.

How to handle a request:

  1. Read the Employee, Leave Type, From / To dates, Days, and Reason columns.
  2. Click the gold Approve button if you're happy. The request will be forwarded to admin/MD for final sign-off.
  3. Click the red Reject button if it can't be granted. You'll be asked to type a brief reason — be respectful; the employee sees it.
Important: Your approval is the first stage. After you approve, it goes to admin for final approval. Until both stages are done, the employee's leave balance isn't deducted.

📊Team Performance

Performance scores for everyone in your department.

What you see: Each team member has an overall score out of 100, broken into six metrics: Attendance, Punctuality, Consistency, Lateness, Manager Rating, and Skills. Green ≥ 80, Yellow 60–79, Red below 60.

What you can do here:

  • Open someone's profile to see the detail behind their score.
  • Use the Review button to give them a star rating and written comments — this is the "Manager Rating" used in their overall score.
  • Use the Skills button to rate them on individual skills (communication, teamwork, etc.). Skill ratings drive 40% of their overall score.
Why this matters: Skills and Manager Rating together are 60% of the score. If you don't fill these in, your team members will appear to underperform — even if their attendance is perfect.
Part 2 — Your Own Account

⏰My Time Logging

Same as any employee: record when you started, when you broke, and when you finished each working day.

The system uses these entries to calculate your own Consistency and Lateness scores.

📅My Attendance

Sign In when you arrive, Sign Out when you leave. Just like any employee.

Signing in here also automatically fills your Start Time in My Time Logging, so you don't have to enter it twice.

🌴My Leave Requests

Submit your own time off. As a Lead, your request goes straight to admin/MD for approval (skipping the first lead-approval step, since you're it).

How: Pick dates, choose leave type, write a reason, click Submit. Your remaining balance appears at the top.

📈My Performance

Your own overall score, broken down the same way as your team members'.

Frequently Asked Questions

I clicked Approve but nothing seems to have happened.

You should see a confirmation alert pop up. If you don't, the request couldn't be forwarded — usually because there is no active admin or MD account in the system. Check with IT.

Can I approve someone outside my department?

No. Leave Approvals only shows requests from your own department. If you need to cover for another lead, ask an admin to reassign.

What happens after I approve?

The request moves to the admin's queue. Until they approve it, the employee's leave balance is NOT deducted. Once admin approves, the balance is deducted and the days are auto-marked as Leave in attendance.

I rejected a request by mistake. Can I undo it?

Not directly — the employee will need to resubmit. Send them a quick message so they know to submit again.

How do I rate skills?

Open Team Performance, click the Skills button next to a team member's name, and rate them 1–5 on each predefined skill. Save when done.

Someone's overall score is red but I think they're doing fine.

Open their record and look at each metric. The most common cause is missing data — they may not have skill ratings, a manager review, or time logs yet. Fill those in and the score will rise.
