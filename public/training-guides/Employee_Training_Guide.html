1. Welcome — what TaskTango is for

TaskTango is the place where your company tracks who's working, who's on leave, who's signed in today, and how everyone's performing. It replaces the old habit of WhatsApping your manager to say "I've arrived" and emailing HR for a leave form — everything happens in one app.

As an employee, you'll use TaskTango to:

  • Sign in at the start of each work day and sign out at the end
  • Log your breaks so your real working hours are accurate
  • Request leave (annual, sick, bereavement, etc.) and see how much you have left
  • Chat & call teammates without needing a separate app
  • Read your performance reviews when your lead writes one
  • See company structure (who's the lead of which team)
  • Download your HR documents (offer letter, contracts) any time you need them
TaskTango runs in two flavours: a desktop app installed on your work PC, and a web version you can open in any browser at the URL your admin gave you. Both look identical and your account works on either.

2. Logging in for the first time

Step-by-step

  1. Open TaskTango (double-click the desktop icon, or go to the URL your admin emailed you).
  2. You'll see a green login screen. Type the email address your admin set up for you.
  3. Type the temporary password you were given.
  4. Click Sign In. The app will immediately ask you to choose a new password — this is mandatory on first login.
  5. Pick something at least 6 characters long. Confirm it. Click Save.
  6. You're in.
Your temporary password only works once. If you don't get to the password-change step (e.g., you closed the browser by accident), ask your admin to reset your password — they can issue a fresh temporary one.
HR creates an account for "Priya Sharma" with email priya@acme.com and a one-time password Welcome123. Priya logs in on Monday morning, sees the change-password prompt, and sets her real password to something she'll remember. From Tuesday onwards she uses only her real password.

If you forget your password later

You can't reset it yourself — that's deliberate, so nobody else can hijack your account from a "forgot password" form. Just message your admin (or open a chat with them inside TaskTango if you can get in from another device) and they'll reset it in seconds.

Auto-logout after inactivity

If you walk away for 30 minutes without touching the keyboard or mouse, TaskTango logs you out automatically. You'll get a brief warning toast, then the login screen. This protects your account if you leave a workstation unattended.

3. Your dashboard — a tour

The first thing you see after logging in is My Dashboard. It has three main zones:

The header bar (top of the page)

  • Quick Sign In/Out chip — a small pill that shows your status for today: ▶ Sign In if you haven't started, ● In · 09:14 once you've signed in, ■ Done · 17:42 after you've signed out. Click it to sign in/out without leaving the page.
  • Chat icon 💬 — opens the chat sidebar. A red bubble appears when you have unread messages.
  • Notification bell 🔔 — leave approvals, new reviews, system messages.
  • Your avatar — your photo if uploaded, otherwise the first letter of your name on an orange circle.

The left sidebar (your menu)

  • Dashboard — this page
  • Employee Directory — see everyone in the company (read-only for you)
  • Time Logging — start/end your day and break tracking
  • Attendance — your calendar of present/absent/leave days
  • Leave Requests — apply for time off, see balances
  • My Performance — view reviews your lead has written about you
  • My Documents — your offer letter, contracts, ID copies
  • Org Chart — visual company structure with PDF export

The main content area

This shows your profile card on the left (with a profile completeness ring — the green donut tells you how much of your profile is filled in) and four quick-stat cards on the right:

  • Log Your Time — jumps to Time Logging
  • Annual Leave Balance — how many days you have left this year
  • Punctuality — derived from your last 30 days of sign-ins
  • Performance Score — your most recent review average
The profile completeness ring turns green at 90 %+, amber at 60–89 %, and red below 60 %. A complete profile means HR has everything they need to process payroll, send statutory notices, etc. If yours is red, scroll down to Section 12 and fill in the blanks.

4. Signing in & signing out (the daily routine)

This is the most important thing you'll do every day. There are two equally valid ways to do it:

Option A — the Quick chip in the header (recommended)

  1. When you arrive at work and open TaskTango, look at the top-right.
  2. You'll see ▶ Sign In.
  3. Click it once. The chip turns green and shows ● In · 09:14.
  4. When you finish for the day, click the green chip. It changes to ■ Done · 17:42.

Option B — the Attendance page

  1. Open Attendance from the sidebar.
  2. Click the big Sign In button at the top.
  3. At the end of the day, return and click Sign Out.
If you forget to sign in within 30 minutes of your scheduled start time, the system will automatically mark you Absent for the day. You can still sign in afterwards, but the absent flag stays unless your admin clears it. Sign in as soon as you start work.
If you forget to sign out, an automatic cron at 23:00 (office time) will sign you out using your contracted end-time. Your hours may not match what you actually worked — message your lead to correct the record.
Arjun's contracted day is 09:00–18:00 IST. He arrives at 09:05 Monday, taps the green chip → signed in at 09:05. He has lunch from 13:00–14:00 and remembers to log it as a Break on the Time Logging page (next section). He signs out at 18:30. His timesheet shows: Worked 8h 25m, Break 1h, Net 7h 25m.

5. Time logging — start, breaks, end

The Time Logging page is where the detail of your day lives. Sign In and Sign Out (Section 4) cover the bookends; this page also tracks breaks.

The three buttons

ButtonWhen to press itWhat it records
▶ Start BreakWhen you step away — lunch, coffee, school pickupA "break started" timestamp
⏸ End BreakWhen you're back at your deskA "break ended" timestamp; the gap is added to your total break time
📋 HistoryTo look at past daysA scrollable list of every day you've logged
You can log multiple breaks in one day. The page totals them up — 15-min morning tea + 45-min lunch + 10-min coffee = 70 minutes of break.

Viewing your history

The history table shows: Date · Sign In · Sign Out · Worked · Break · Net hours. As an employee you can only view — the Edit and Delete buttons are hidden because tampering with your own timesheet would defeat the point. Only admins can correct entries.

Export your hours to PDF

At the top of the History tab there's a 📄 Export to PDF button. It produces a clean monthly timesheet you can email to HR or keep for your records.

Sneha sees that yesterday's "Sign Out" is missing because the auto-cron signed her out at 23:00 (she forgot to click). The system shows 13 hours worked, which is wrong. She messages her admin: "Hey, please correct yesterday's sign-out to 18:15." Admin fixes it in 30 seconds.

6. Attendance — checking your record

The Attendance page shows a monthly calendar of your status each day. Click any day for details.

Status colours you'll see

  • Present — you signed in
  • Late — you signed in after the 30-min grace window
  • Absent — no sign-in at all that day
  • On Leave — an approved leave covered this day
  • Holiday — public holiday set by admin
  • Weekend — Saturday/Sunday by default

The page also shows monthly totals at the top: Present days, Leave days, Late days, Absent days. Use these to sanity-check before your manager does at month-end.

7. Requesting time off

How to submit a request

  1. Open Leave Requests from the sidebar.
  2. At the top you'll see your balance cards — one per leave type your company offers (Annual, Sick, Casual, etc.). Each card shows: Total allocated · Used · Remaining.
  3. Click + New Leave Request.
  4. Choose the leave type (Annual / Sick / etc.).
  5. Pick the Start date and End date from the calendar.
  6. Write a short reason — be specific enough that your lead can decide ("Family wedding in Pune", "GP appointment", "Annual holiday").
  7. Click Submit. The request now sits in your lead's queue.

What happens after you submit

The two-stage approval flow

Every leave request goes through up to two checkpoints:

  1. Department lead approves → status changes to "Lead approved"
  2. Admin gives final approval → status becomes "Approved" and the days are deducted from your balance

If your department has no lead, the request skips straight to admin. You'll see a notification in your 🔔 bell at each stage.

Cancelling a request

Click any pending request in your list, then click Cancel Request. You can cancel any request that hasn't been approved yet. Once approved, you can only cancel if your admin allows it (and the days will be returned to your balance).

Exporting upcoming leaves to your calendar

On the Leave Requests page, look for the 📅 Export to Calendar button. Click it and TaskTango downloads an .ics file containing all your approved upcoming leaves. Open this file with Google Calendar, Outlook, or Apple Calendar and your time-off appears as all-day events.

Maria gets a wedding invite for 12–14 March. On 1 March she opens Leave Requests, clicks + New, picks Annual Leave, 12 Mar – 14 Mar, writes "Cousin's wedding in Goa", submits. Her lead approves at 11:00, admin approves at 14:00, and her bell tings twice. On 5 March she exports the .ics, opens it in Outlook, and her boss can see the OOO in the shared team calendar.

8. My Performance — reading your review

When your lead or admin writes a performance review about you, it appears under My Performance. You can:

  • Read the scores across the categories your company uses (e.g. Punctuality, Quality of Work, Teamwork, Initiative)
  • See your overall rating as "X / 5"
  • Read the lead's comments
  • Download a polished PDF of your review for your personal records
The PDF includes your name, your team lead's name, the review period, scores, comments, and a signature line. Keep a copy — it's useful when you negotiate a raise or job-change.

If you disagree with a review

Talk to your lead first. If you can't resolve it, send a message via the in-app chat to your admin requesting a meeting. TaskTango doesn't currently have a formal "dispute" button — the conversation happens out of the app.

9. My Documents — HR papers on file

This is the simplest page in the app. It lists every document HR has uploaded for you: offer letter, signed contract, copy of your ID/passport, address proof, NDA — whatever they keep on file.

What you can do here

  • Download any document by clicking the row — it opens or saves the file (PDF, JPG, etc.)
  • See when it was uploaded and what type it is

What you can't do here

  • You cannot upload documents yourself — HR controls this. If you need to update an address proof, email it to HR and they'll replace the file.
  • You cannot delete documents — that's an admin-only action.

10. Org Chart — who works where

The Org Chart shows the company in tree form: Managing Director / Admin at the top, Department Leads on the next level, and team members below their respective leads.

What you can do

  • Zoom in / out using the + / − buttons
  • Pan around by clicking and dragging the empty space
  • See photos — if someone has uploaded a profile picture it shows in the node
  • Export to PDF — click the ⬇ Export to PDF button. The chart prints to A3 landscape, perfect for the office wall.
Karan just joined and isn't sure who his lead's lead is. He opens Org Chart, finds his own name, traces the line upward — his lead is Anjali (Engineering), her lead is Rohit (CTO, marked as MD). Now he knows the chain of command for escalations.

11. Chat, calls & video

TaskTango has a built-in chat (like WhatsApp or Slack), plus 1-to-1 audio and video calls. No external app needed.

Opening chat

Click the 💬 icon in the top header. A panel slides in from the right. On the left of that panel is the contacts list, on the right is the active conversation.

Understanding the presence dots

Next to every name (and on every avatar) you'll see a small coloured dot indicating what that person is doing right now:

DotMeansBest time to message?
GreenSigned in and actively workingYes — they're at their desk
AmberOn a breakYes, but expect a slight delay
YellowSigned in but inactive for > 10 minSend a message; they'll see it when back
BlueSigned out for the dayThey'll see it tomorrow
RedMarked absent todayNot at work — try email
PurpleOn approved leaveDon't disturb unless urgent
GreyHasn't been seen in a whileStatus unknown

Sending a message

  1. Click a name in the contacts list to open a thread.
  2. Type in the box at the bottom.
  3. Press Enter to send. Use Shift+Enter for a new line within the same message.
  4. Click the 😀 icon to add an emoji.
  5. Click the 📎 icon to attach a file (image, PDF, doc — up to your admin's size limit).

Making a call

Inside any conversation, at the top of the thread you'll see two buttons:

  • 🎤 Audio Call — starts a voice call
  • 🎥 Video Call — starts a video call

The other person hears a ringtone and sees an "Incoming call" banner. They can Accept or Decline. If they decline or don't pick up, a "Missed call" line appears in the chat.

The minimize button (so calls don't take over your screen)

During a call you'll see a small ➖ button in the call controls. Click it and the video shrinks to a tiny floating window (bottom-right of the screen). You can now keep using TaskTango — read emails, open the Org Chart, whatever — while the call continues. Click ⛶ to bring it back to full size.

Calls go peer-to-peer using WebRTC. The call doesn't pass through TaskTango's server, so quality depends on your and the other person's internet. If a call is choppy, switching from video to audio-only usually helps.
First time you make a call from the browser version, you'll see a "Allow microphone / camera" permission prompt. Click Allow — if you click Block, no calls will work until you reset the permission in browser settings.

12. Updating your profile

From the Employee Directory, find your own name and click View. You'll see your profile. Some fields you can edit yourself; others (job title, salary, department) are admin-only.

Fields you can update

  • Profile picture — click the avatar and upload a square JPG/PNG (under 2 MB recommended)
  • Phone number — emergency contact
  • Address
  • Bank account & IFSC — for payroll
  • Date of birth
After updating, your profile completeness ring on the dashboard recalculates. Aim for 100 % so you never get a payroll problem or a missed-birthday gift.

14. FAQ & troubleshooting

Q: I can't sign in — it says "Invalid credentials"

Check Caps Lock. If that's not it, the password's been reset (or never set) — message your admin.

Q: I clicked Sign In but it flickered and went back to the button

The page may not have grabbed today's correct date. Refresh (F5 or Ctrl+R) and try again. If it still happens, your office timezone setting may be wrong — tell your admin.

Q: I logged a break by mistake

You can't undo it yourself. Ask your admin to delete the entry — they have the Edit/Delete buttons.

Q: Why does my leave balance look wrong?

Open the Leave Requests page once — that "re-seeds" your balance row from current settings. If still off, your admin can correct it in the Employee Manager.

Q: Chat says someone's green but they're not responding

Green means signed in, not "actively at the keyboard". They could be in a meeting. After 10 minutes of inactivity the dot turns yellow.

Q: I joined a call but the other person can't hear me

Check that your browser/Electron has microphone permission. In Chrome: lock icon in address bar → Site settings → Microphone → Allow. Then leave and rejoin the call.

Q: How do I know which version I'm on?

Look at the bottom-right corner of any page — there's a small badge that says Build Production v4.5. Click it for an About box with full version details.

Q: Where is my data stored? Is it safe?

If you're on the desktop app, it's on your work PC. If you're on the web version, it's on your company's TaskTango server. Either way, your admin controls backups. Passwords are stored hashed (bcrypt) — even an admin cannot read your raw password.

Q: I'm leaving the company — what happens to my data?

Your admin will offboard you. Your historical records (timesheets, leaves, reviews) are kept for compliance, but your account is disabled — you can't log in. If you want a copy of your reviews, download the PDFs before your last day.
